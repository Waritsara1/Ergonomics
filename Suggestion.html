Lifting Posture



STEP ONE
Position your feet apart to suit your body
and what you are about to do.
Do not keep your feet together, not even your heels.



STEP TWO
Brace your abdominals.
Brace your shoulders and trunk.
Maintain your lumbar curve.
Incline your trunk forward, hips and knees bent, push bottom backwards so that shoulders are over the centre of gravity of the load.

STEP THREE
Test the weight of the load. Lift one side of the object.
(if itâ€™s too heavy or awkward, stop and get help)
Keep the load close to your body with your back,abdominals,shoulders and elbows braced.



STEP FOUR
Keep your head and chest up.
Straighten up by extending hips and knees.
Change direction with your feet: avoid twisting while bent or loaded.





STEP FIVE
To lower the load to the floor, reverse the process, making sure you maintain the lumbar curve and bracing.



Before You Lift



PLAN AHEAD
1.Know what you are lifting and how you will lift it.
2.Be aware of the weight of the object.
3.Determine whether or not it's safe to lift on your own.
4.Make sure the work area is flat,dry and clear of debris.
CHECK YOUR PATHWAY
1.Make sure the lift pathway is clear.
2.Remove any tripping hazard or debris.
3.Check for any wet or slick surfaces.
USE ERGONOMIC EQUIPMENT
1.Use lift assists,forklift,dolly,cart,hand truck or hoist.
2.Make sure you are trained trained before using the equipment
GET HELP WHEN NEEDED
1.When lifting awkward or heavy loads,utilize a two person lift.
2.Make sure you lift at the same time and keep the load level.
WEAR PROPER PPE
Wear proper required protective shoes and gloves.

Lifting Do's


- Know or test the object wegiht.
- Use ergonomic lift assists when possible.
- Plan the lift and clear your path.
- Get help for heavy or awkward loads.
- Use a wide stance for balance.
- Use your legs to lift.
- Pivot your feet to avoid twisting
